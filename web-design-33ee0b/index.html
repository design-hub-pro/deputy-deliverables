How Landscapes Carry the Traces of Time

景观如何承载时间的痕迹

Landscapes carry time in different ways. Glacial deposits preserve the history of the Earth. Water remembers the paths it has followed. Forests and wetlands record ecological continuity, while roads and neighbourhoods leave new marks on the land. Land Memory Lab uses GIS, long-term public data, and visual storytelling to explore how these traces remain, fragment, and take new forms across time.

景观以不同方式承载时间。冰川沉积物保存着地球形成的历史，水记得自己流经过的路径，森林与湿地记录着生态系统的延续，道路和社区则在土地上留下新的印记。土地记忆实验室通过 GIS、长期公共数据与动态视觉叙事，探索这些痕迹如何在时间中持续、破碎，并形成新的形态。

The Framework

How Landscapes Remember土地记忆的研究框架

Different lands preserve history in different ways. Land Memory Lab reads a place as a stack of overlapping records — geological, hydrological, ecological, and human — and asks which of those records are still legible in the landscape today.

不同的土地以不同方式保存历史。土地记忆实验室把一个地方视为多层叠加的记录——地质的、水文的、生态的与人类的——并追问：在今天的景观中，哪些记录仍然可以被读出来。

Classification

Three Types of Land Memory三类土地记忆

Every parcel in the study area is assigned to one of three classes, based on how its natural cover and spatial context changed between the first and last time slice.

Persistent

Persistent Memory持续记忆

Wetlands, streams, woodlands or natural corridors that have continued from 2002 to today, with the surrounding land structure remaining relatively intact.

湿地、溪流、林地或自然走廊从 2002 年持续至今，周边土地结构保持相对完整。

Fragmented

Fragmented Memory破碎记忆

The natural feature still exists, but roads, buildings or urban expansion have weakened its original spatial connectivity.

自然特征仍然存在，但道路、建筑或城市扩张削弱了原有的空间连通性。

Transformed

Transformed Memory重写记忆

Former natural or open land has become built land, and the relationship between soil, water and vegetation has been reshaped.

过去的自然或开放土地转变为城市用地，土地、水与植被之间的关系被重新塑造。

Why Aurora

A Small Town With a Complete Record

Aurora sits where three landscape systems meet: the East Holland River, Tannery Creek, and the southern flank of the Oak Ridges Moraine. It is small enough to study end-to-end, and documented well enough — through the Lake Simcoe Region Conservation Authority, the Province of Ontario, and the Town’s own data hub — to make a twenty-one-year comparison genuinely verifiable.

Aurora 处在三个景观系统的交汇处：East Holland River、Tannery Creek 与 Oak Ridges Moraine 南缘。它足够小，可以被完整研究；同时公共数据足够完善，使二十一年的对比分析真正可验证。

Case Study 01

Beneath AuroraWater, Vegetation, and the Stories Written into the Land

Using official GIS data from 2002, 2013 and 2023, Beneath Aurora examines how the town’s natural vegetation, wetlands, water corridors and built land have changed — and which land memories are still visible in the landscape today. The study area is the Aurora municipal boundary together with the natural areas connected to the East Holland River, Tannery Creek and the Oak Ridges Moraine.

研究范围包括 Aurora 市界，以及与 East Holland River、Tannery Creek 和 Oak Ridges Moraine 相连的主要自然区域。

Water Memory

Where the Water Still Runs水的记忆

A 100-metre buffer is generated around every mapped watercourse and wetland. Within that buffer, the platform tracks how much land stayed natural, how much became impervious, and where the buffer was crossed by new roads — the clearest signal of a water corridor losing its continuity.

在所有已制图的溪流与湿地周围生成 100 米缓冲区，追踪其中自然土地的保留、硬化地表的增加，以及新道路的穿越——水系走廊失去连续性最直接的信号。

Context: LSRCA reports that impervious cover across the Lake Simcoe watershed reached 4.3% in 2013, having increased since 2002. Aurora-specific values are what this study computes.

Living Memory

What the Vegetation Kept生命 / 植被的记忆

Forest, woodland, meadow, shrub and wetland vegetation are compared patch by patch across the three time slices. Retention answers whether a patch survived; connectivity and edge density answer whether it survived whole. A woodlot can lose none of its area and still lose most of what made it a corridor.

逐个斑块比较三个年份的森林、林地、草地、灌木与湿地植被。保留率回答斑块是否存续；连通性与边缘密度回答它是否完整地存续——一片林地可以面积不变，却失去作为走廊的全部功能。

What Lies Beneath

The Oldest Memory in AuroraAurora 地下的记忆

Beneath the land-cover layers sits the deepest record of all: glacial sand, gravel, silt, clay and till left by the ice, and the Oak Ridges Moraine recharge system that still moves water through them. Surficial geology, elevation and hillshade explain why wetlands sit where they sit — and why some parts of Aurora were always going to stay wet.

土地覆盖之下是最古老的记录：冰川留下的砂、砾、粉砂、黏土与冰碛，以及至今仍在其中输送水的 Oak Ridges Moraine 补给系统。表层地质、高程与山体阴影解释了湿地为何出现在它们所在的位置。

Analytical Workflow分析方法

1

Define the study area

Clip all layers to the Aurora municipal boundary plus connected natural areas along the East Holland River, Tannery Creek and the Oak Ridges Moraine.

2

Harmonise the classes

Reclassify each year’s land cover into the four analysis categories so that 2002, 2013 and 2023 are directly comparable.

3

Align projection & resolution

Reproject to a common CRS and resample to a shared grid so that per-cell change detection is valid.

4

Change detection

Build 2002→2013, 2013→2023 and 2002→2023 transition matrices for every category pair.

5

Water-corridor buffers

Generate 100 m buffers around streams, waterbodies and wetlands; compute natural, impervious and built proportions inside each buffer per year.

6

Fragmentation metrics

Compute patch count, mean patch size, edge density and road-crossing counts to separate area loss from connectivity loss.

7

Classify land memory

Assign each area to Persistent, Fragmented or Transformed using retention, connectivity and buffer-change thresholds.

8

Add the deep layers

Overlay surficial geology, HRDEM elevation and the ORM boundary to explain why the observed pattern occurs where it does.

9

Ground-truth & narrate

Visit the four story sites, photograph them, and compare what the data claims with what the place — and its residents — record.

Limitations & Honest Caveats研究限制

  • Photo-year nuance. Watershed-wide air photo coverage for the earliest slice spans 2002–2003; “2002” in this study means that coverage epoch, not a single date.
  • Classification drift. Mapping methods and minimum mapping units changed between coverages. Apparent change near class boundaries may be methodological rather than real, so results are reported at patch level rather than per-pixel.
  • Three snapshots are not a continuous record. Land that was cleared and regrew — or was disturbed and restored — between slices is invisible to this design.
  • A 100 m buffer is a convention, not an ecological threshold. It is used for comparability; sensitivity is tested at 30 m and 250 m.
  • Correlation, not causation. The platform documents spatial change; it does not attribute that change to specific policy or development decisions.
  • Personal and local memory is testimony, not measurement. Place stories are presented as a distinct kind of evidence, never merged into the quantitative layers.
Future Studies

The Method Is Built to TravelWhitehorse、Inuvik 及未来案例方向

Aurora is a controlled first case: small, well-documented, and close enough to visit. But the underlying question — what does a landscape remember? — matters most where the record is changing fastest.

Case 02 · Proposed

Whitehorse, Yukon

A northern city on a major river system, where valley terraces, boreal forest and rapid municipal growth intersect. Tests whether the memory classification holds in a subarctic, low-density setting.

Case 03 · Proposed

Inuvik, Northwest Territories

Where land memory is stored in permafrost. Thaw ponds, subsidence and shifting drainage make the traces of time visible on a decade scale rather than a century one — and connect directly to Edward’s permafrost modelling work.
