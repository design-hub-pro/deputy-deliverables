The challenge

Support for autistic people is scattered, clinical, and hard to trust.

Families spend months piecing together fragments from forums, PDFs, and waiting lists. Autistic adults face a hidden unemployment rate above 70%, even when they are highly capable. And most "support" websites overwhelm the very people they claim to serve — with cluttered layouts, harsh colors, and jarging motion.

Fragmented information

Reliable, evidence-based guidance is buried across dozens of sites, written in clinical language that families struggle to act on.

Barriers to employment

Talented autistic candidates are filtered out by rigid interviews and workplaces that were never designed with neurodivergent minds in mind.

Isolation & overload

Community spaces are noisy and inaccessible, while sensory-heavy design pushes autistic users away from the resources meant for them.

From student project to movement

MindBridge AI began as a question, not a company.

It started with a simple observation: the technology that could make life dramatically easier for autistic people already exists — it just isn't pointed at them with care. What began as a research project into accessible, AI-assisted navigation for autism resources grew into a full nonprofit platform.

  • Phase 1 — Listen. Interviews with autistic self-advocates, parents, and educators shaped every design decision.
  • Phase 2 — Build. A sensory-friendly platform uniting knowledge, marketplace, jobs, and community.
  • Phase 3 — Scale. An AI assistant and employer network turning a prototype into lasting infrastructure.

About the founder

"I didn't want to build another app that talks about autistic people. I wanted to build a place that listens to them — and then gets out of the way."

Yu Sun founded MindBridge AI after watching families they love navigate a system that made support feel like a maze. With a background bridging computer science and human-centered design, Yu leads the platform with a single rule: nothing about autistic people is decided without autistic people in the room.

Today Yu works alongside a volunteer team of clinicians, educators, engineers, and self-advocates to keep MindBridge AI free, calm, and genuinely useful.
